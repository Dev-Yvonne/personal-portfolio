Frequently Asked Questions

Get answers to common questions about mental health

When should I seek professional help?

Consider seeking professional help if you experience persistent sadness, anxiety that interferes with daily life, suicidal thoughts, substance abuse, or any condition that affects your relationships or work. There's no \"right time\" - reaching out early is always beneficial.

Is meditation difficult for beginners?

Not at all! Meditation is a skill that improves with practice. Our guided meditations are designed for all levels. Even 3-5 minutes daily can make a difference. Don't worry about a \"perfect\" meditation - just focus on being present.

How does exercise help mental health?

Exercise releases endorphins (feel-good chemicals), reduces stress hormones, improves sleep, and increases self-confidence. Even 20-30 minutes of activity can significantly improve mood and mental clarity.

Can I manage mental health without medication?

Yes, lifestyle changes like exercise, meditation, therapy, and social support can be very effective. However, some conditions benefit from medication. The best approach is personalized - work with your healthcare provider to find what works for you.

How often should I track my mood?

Daily tracking is ideal to identify patterns, but even tracking 3-4 times weekly provides valuable insights. Consistency matters more than frequency. Use it as a tool for self-awareness, not as a burden.

Is it okay to talk about mental health?

Absolutely! Talking about mental health reduces stigma and helps others feel less alone. You don't need to share with everyone, but confiding in trusted friends, family, or professionals is healthy and encouraged.

Mental Health Tips

Simple daily practices for better mental wellbeing

1

Practice Gratitude

Write down 3 things you're grateful for each day. This simple practice rewires your brain toward positivity and increases happiness.

2

Set Boundaries

Learn to say no to protect your mental energy. Healthy boundaries are essential for maintaining emotional wellbeing and relationships.

3

Stay Active

Exercise regularly - even a 20-minute walk releases endorphins. Physical activity is a proven mood booster and stress reliever.

4

Connect with Others

Spend quality time with supportive friends and family. Social connection is crucial for mental health and emotional resilience.

5

Practice Mindfulness

Be present in the moment and observe thoughts without judgment. Mindfulness reduces anxiety and increases overall wellbeing.

6

Seek Help

Don't hesitate to reach out to professionals. Therapy and counseling are signs of strength, not weakness.

7

Keep a Journal

Writing helps process emotions and track mental patterns. Journaling is a therapeutic tool used by therapists worldwide.

8

Limit Screen Time

Reduce exposure to negative news and social media. Taking digital breaks improves mental clarity and reduces anxiety.
